Section 02 · Strengths

What Works Well ✅

Despite the design gaps, the NayePankh website has a genuine foundation to build on. Here's what deserves recognition:

🏛️
Trust Signals are Present
The site clearly displays UP Govt registration, 80G & 12A certification, and newspaper recognitions (Pioneer, Dainik Jagran, Hindustan). These are powerful credibility markers that many NGO sites miss entirely.
📖
Authentic Origin Story
The About Us page tells a heartfelt story of starting during COVID-19, founded by high schoolers. This genuinely sets the org apart. Real, emotional storytelling is hard to manufacture.
🌱
Mission is Clear
The core message — uplifting the underprivileged through food, sanitary pads, clothes, and education — is consistently communicated across pages. No ambiguity about what the org does.
📲
Social Media Presence
Instagram, LinkedIn, YouTube, Facebook, and Twitter links are all present in the footer. The foundation has understood that social proof extends beyond the website itself.
🧾
Tax Exemption Mentioned
The site mentions 80G tax benefits to donors — a key motivator for Indian donors that many NGO sites forget to highlight prominently.
📞
Contact Info is Visible
Phone number (+91-8318500748) and email (contact@nayepankh.com) appear on multiple pages, making it easy to reach out — a basic but often forgotten essential.
Section 03 · Design & UX Issues

What Needs Improvement 🔧

The following are the most critical issues, ordered by impact. Each includes a "before vs after" view of the recommendation.

01
Replace WhatsApp Images with Professional Photography
Every image on the site appears to be exported from WhatsApp, visible from the compressed quality and filename hints in the CDN URLs. This is the single biggest credibility drain. For an NGO that works with 30,000+ interns and has media coverage, professional event photos should be accessible.
Currently
Low-resolution WhatsApp group photos used as hero and section backgrounds. Pixelated on desktop screens.
Recommended
Curated high-resolution event photos, compressed properly via tools like TinyPNG. Hero should show volunteers with beneficiaries.
02
Fix Spelling Errors Immediately
A button on the homepage reads "OUR CERITIFICATES" — a glaring typo for an NGO that presents itself as professional and government-certified. There are also grammatical inconsistencies across the About page. These erode trust instantly.
Currently
"OUR CERITIFICATES" button on homepage. Several informal sentence constructions on About page.
Recommended
Run all text through Grammarly, fix all typos, and adopt consistent, professional tone. Quick 30-minute fix.
03
Redesign the Donation Page — It Has No Donate Button
The most critical page for an NGO is the donate page. Currently it has a long emotional letter, some photos, and text saying "Click on the button below to donate to Us" — but no working payment gateway or a clear, visible button with an amount. This directly kills conversions.
Currently
Long letter of appeal, no donation amounts shown, no payment method options visible, no "donate ₹100 / ₹500 / ₹1000" quick options.
Recommended
Prominent preset amounts (₹100, ₹500, ₹1000, Custom), Razorpay/UPI integration, a progress bar showing this month's goal, and 80G receipt mention.
04
Add a Real Impact Counter with Live Numbers
The homepage mentions "2 lakh people helped" only in text. Animated counter widgets showing meals distributed, interns trained, and clothes donated would create immediate emotional impact and demonstrate scale. Organisations like GiveIndia and Milaap use this effectively.
Currently
Impact numbers mentioned in paragraph text only. Easily missed while scrolling.
Recommended
Animated counter section: "2,00,000+ People Helped · 30,000 Interns · 3 Cities · Since 2021" displayed as large, bold, scroll-triggered numbers.
05
Redesign Navigation — Too Cluttered & Inconsistent
The navbar has "NewsPaper-Recognition" as a page name (inconsistent capitalisation, hyphen in URL looks unprofessional). The Join Us link on the homepage actually goes to the Donate page — confusing for users who want to volunteer rather than donate.
Currently
5-item nav with "NewsPaper-Recognition" and a broken "Join Us" CTA that redirects to Donate. No search. No hamburger menu on mobile.
Recommended
Clean nav: Home · About · Our Work · Internships · Donate (highlighted). Separate "Volunteer" and "Donate" flows entirely. Mobile hamburger menu.
06
Typography & Colour System Needs a Refresh
The site uses default Hostinger/Zyrosite fonts with no deliberate typographic hierarchy. Headers, body text, and CTAs feel undifferentiated. There's no consistent brand colour palette — red, white, and generic sans-serif doesn't signal "trusted NGO," it signals "default template."
Currently
Default builder fonts, red CTA buttons that clash with a serious NGO context, no defined brand palette.
Recommended
Deep navy (#1a1a2e) + warm white + sky blue accent. Inter for body, a humanist serif for headings. Conveys trust and warmth simultaneously.
Section 04 · New Sections & Features

What Should Be Added 🆕

The current site is missing several pages and features that NGOs of this scale and credibility should have. Here are the highest-priority additions:

📊
Live Impact Dashboard
High Priority
A dedicated page showing real-time or regularly updated impact metrics: meals distributed, drives conducted, interns trained, cities covered, and year-wise growth. Include downloadable annual impact reports. This builds donor confidence and attracts CSR partnerships.
🎓
Internship & Volunteer Portal
High Priority
A structured internship application page with eligibility criteria, program duration, expected outcomes, certificate preview, and an online application form. NayePankh has trained 30,000+ interns — this is a major recruitment channel that currently has no dedicated home. Include a searchable intern testimonials section.
📸
Gallery / Media Archive
Medium Priority
A filterable media gallery showing events by category: food drives, clothes distribution, menstrual hygiene awareness, education campaigns, and animal welfare. Organised by date and city. This gives prospective donors and interns a visual proof-of-work that text alone cannot provide.
🗞️
Blog / Stories Section
Medium Priority
A blog where interns share their experiences, the team publishes drive summaries, and success stories of beneficiaries are documented. This creates SEO value (more indexed pages), emotional connection for visitors, and a living archive of the organisation's growth. LinkedIn already shows intern testimonials — bring them onto the site.
🤝
CSR Partnerships & Sponsor Page
Medium Priority
A dedicated page for corporate social responsibility collaborations. Include sponsorship tiers (Gold, Silver, Bronze), what each level funds, logos of past partners, and a contact form specifically for business enquiries. Many Indian companies have mandatory CSR budgets — this page could unlock significant funding.
📬
Newsletter / Updates Subscription
Low Priority
An email capture for monthly impact updates, upcoming drives, and volunteer opportunities. Building a subscriber list creates a direct communication channel that isn't dependent on social media algorithms. Even a simple Mailchimp-powered form would work.

Section 06 · Recommended Front-End Project

Build This: Intern Impact Tracker 🛠️

If NayePankh were to build one front-end project that could transform their digital presence and operational impact simultaneously, this is it:

⚡ Front-End Web App

NayePankh Intern Dashboard & Public Impact Tracker

A React-based web application (embeddable in the site) that gives each intern a personal dashboard to log their activities, and displays an aggregated public-facing impact tracker on the homepage. It solves three simultaneous problems: intern engagement, data collection, and donor transparency.

🧑‍💼 Intern Login Portal
Interns sign in with a unique ID, log their volunteer activities (meals distributed, drives attended, hours logged), and download their participation certificates automatically.
📊 Public Impact Dashboard
The aggregated data from all interns renders as a live, animated dashboard visible to site visitors — showing total meals, clothes, hygiene kits distributed across all cities and events.
🗺️ City-wise Activity Map
An interactive India map (using Leaflet.js or D3) showing drive locations plotted as pins, with pop-ups showing event details. Donors and media can instantly see geographic reach.
📜 Auto Certificate Generator
After completing required hours/activities, interns get a downloadable PDF certificate auto-generated with their name, NGO seal, and activity summary — reducing admin load by 90%.
Why This Project · Impact Areas
📈 Reduces certificate admin work
🤝 Engages 30K+ interns digitally
💰 Increases donor confidence
🔍 Creates SEO-rich dynamic content

💡 Final Thought

NayePankh Foundation has genuinely remarkable real-world impact — 2 lakh lives touched, 30,000 interns trained, national media coverage, and government certification. The website just hasn't caught up with that story yet. Fixing the foundational issues (images, typos, donations) would take a motivated team less than a week. The bigger vision — an Intern Impact Tracker — could set NayePankh apart as the most digitally transparent student-led NGO in India. The story is already there. The website just needs to tell it properly.
